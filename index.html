About

Overview

Communication breakdown in everyday conversation is a sensitive marker of cognitive decline—but standard clinical tests often miss it.

Alzheimer's disease and related dementias (ADRD), along with broader forms of age-related cognitive decline, are typically assessed using standardized clinical tests. Yet these assessments often fail to capture communication breakdowns that occur in everyday conversational routines—such as difficulty maintaining topics or repairing misunderstandings.1,2 These breakdowns directly affect functioning and independence in the daily lives of older adults.3

As AI caregiving systems like conversational agents become increasingly embedded in the lives of older adults, human–AI interaction provides a new, ecologically valid platform for assessing cognitive function in real-world contexts. These interactions generate conversational data that capture not only what is said, but how communication unfolds over time—revealing underlying patterns that reflect higher-level cognitive function.4 This motivates the urgent need to study interaction-level markers of cognitive change beyond traditional clinical assessments.

This workshop brings together the computational modeling and clinical dementia research communities to identify and translate interaction-level signals of cognitive decline into clinically meaningful insights for caregiving. It aims to foster discussion on how such signals can be modeled as digital indicators and to explore their implications for real-world adaptive communication systems. The workshop spans three complementary areas: NLP, HCI, and clinical neuroscience.

Call for Papers

Topics of Interest

We welcome research papers, position papers, and work in progress on five themes:

01 From Language to Interaction Signals

  • Speech, linguistic, and discourse features as indicators of cognitive decline
  • Acoustic and prosodic signals for detecting early cognitive change
  • Extending language-based assessment from isolated tasks to interactive settings

02 From Interaction to Behavioral Indicators

  • Conversational dynamics (e.g., topic maintenance) as indicators of cognitive function
  • Response latency, repetition, and breakdown patterns in real-world interaction
  • Modeling communication as a dynamic process rather than static performance

03 From Interaction Data to Digital Indicators

  • Computational methods for extracting interpretable interaction-level indicators
  • Longitudinal modeling of cognitive change from continuous interaction data
  • Multimodal integration of language, speech, and behavioral signals
  • Data-driven approaches for interaction-aware cognitive monitoring

04 Translational Implications for Care & Monitoring

  • Interaction-based indicators supporting clinical interpretation and caregiving decisions
  • Integration of interaction-derived signals into clinical and real-world monitoring workflows
  • Passive and continuous monitoring in AI-enabled environments

05 Foundations: Ethics, Fairness & Infrastructure

  • Privacy-preserving methods for continuous cognitive monitoring
  • Fairness and bias across linguistic and demographic populations
  • Datasets and evaluation frameworks for interaction-based cognitive research
  • Ethical and regulatory considerations for AI-assisted cognitive systems
